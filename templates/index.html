Inherent characteristics of a song as a complex sound wave.

443 songs from 35 genres displayed on a "similarity plane." The genres R&B Soul and Alternative Indie are perhaps culturally constructed.




What is this?

The current graph shows 443 songs from the intersection of the 7digital data and the playlist data (see below). The size of each dot is proportional to a popularity score (see below). MP3 preview files were taken from 7digital.com.

The sound waves which make up a specific song can be represented as a multi-dimensional vector. To better visualize these data, we reduced the dimensionality with Principal Component Analysis (PCA). Each data point represents a song plotted on the first two principal components. (arbitrary units)



See for yourself

Hover over each song to listen to a preview. (Some preview clips may be softer and load more slowly.) Songs that are close together on the PCA plane should sound similar. Listen to songs on the extremes of each axis and note any differences or similarities.

Click the genre labels to toggle the display of songs categorized in each genre by 7digital. Double click on the genre label to show songs solely from that genre. Start with hiding all genres except "punk" and "folk". These are the genres that are most distinct.

What it means

We noted that the first principal component (x-axis) represents a soft-to-rock variation. Songs at the left extreme of the axis sound more classical and soft, whereas those on the rightmost extreme sound more hard and rock. The second principal component (y-axis) seems to represent tempo variation. Songs at the bottom extreme of the axis sound slower, whereas those on the topmost extreme sound faster.

Some sets of genres are more distinct than others. The most well-defined distinction was observed between songs classified as pop and those classifed as folk. These distinctions make intuitive sense from experience. Conversely, least well-defined distinction was observed between songs classified as R and B Soul and those classifed as Alternative Indie. These genres may very well be a product of culture - there is not inherent basis for classifying a song as either R and B Soul or Alternative Indie.

Artist interactions performing at the same event.

201 Artists in a collaboration network. The genre-barrier poses few constraints for artist performance collaboration.

What is this?

Each node represents an artist. Nodes are linked if the artists have perfromed together at the same event. The weight of the edges indicates the number of times that connected artists have played at the same event as another artist based on the SongKick Api. The nodes are initially colored by modularity, which are mathematically calculated clusters.



See for yourself

Hover over nodes to view the names of artists. Click and drag to rearrange the network. Use the Color by and Size by options to change the display. Color nodes based on mathematically calcualted modularity (generated without a priori knowledge of artist characteristics). Or, color nodes by genre - the genre in which most songs by that artist are classified. Size nodes based on Page Rank. Or, size nodes by Popularity Score. We have less data on each artist's popularity.

What it means

Artists in the same modularity (calculated with Gephi) perfrom more often together. However, it was surprising that the genre categorizations did not appear clustered in the same netwrok. This may be because we have too many genre calssifications - some genres could really be grouped into one. This may point towards the fact that many artists choose to collaborate with others outside of thier genre. There seems to be two classes or popular artists. Some, such as Survivor, prefer to perform alone - they appear on the sides of the collaboration network. Others, such as The Black Eyed Peas, have performed with many other artists. It is interesting to note how these two styles can both accheive popularity.

What it means

Songs in the same modularity (calculated with Gephi) are more often put together in the same playlist. It appears that there is little correspondance between the calculated clusters and the genres. This may be because we have too many genre calssifications - some genres could really be grouped into one. This may also point towards the fact that many user prefer to put songs of many different genres in a playlist. As expected, the most popular songs cluster in the middle because many user should all list them, and they would be linked to many other songs in many different playlists. Furthermore, as expected, nodes that are most central (calcated by closeness centrality) also appear in the center of the network.

Conclusions

Firstly, we have shown that we can determine some genres using inherent characteristics of song sound waves. With this method, the difference between some genres are more distictive than others. Secondly, we have explored artist interactions, showing that artist do not necessary cluster by genre as expected, and that popular artists may either choose to collaborate or be lone wolves. Lastly, we have shown that user prefer many different genres in their playlists and that popular songs are in the center of the user preference song network.

This project can have many benefits. It can help artists and producers in the music industry produce songs that are more likely to be in the top 40 category, thus increasing revenue. This project can enhance current methods of categorizing and classifying songs, thus making music management/organization software more effective. And finally, it can help individuals find songs that will appeal to their tastes or moods, enhancing their listening experience like never before.
